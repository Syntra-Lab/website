\section{Syntra: Core Context and
Goals}\label{syntra-core-context-and-goals}

Towards Free Agency for All · Technical Architecture · March 2026

\begin{quote}
Syntra is the core engine powering Scholar. It is designed to provide
"Free Agency" through lightweight, edge-first AI agents that run
locally, defend against psychological manipulation, and maintain
constant attention with infinite context.
\end{quote}

\subsection{1. Why Syntra Exists
(Context)}\label{why-syntra-exists-context}

Agency should not be a luxury. The ability to act with clarity, free
from manipulation, informed by intelligence that works for you --- this
should be available to everyone at a reasonably cheap and fair price.

\subsubsection{The Problem Space}\label{the-problem-space}

The modern internet is engineered to exploit human psychology through
dark patterns, engagement traps, and opaque AI systems. Conventional AI
is often:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Server-dependent:} Vulnerable to surveillance and "kill
  switches."
\item
  \textbf{Passive:} Only acts when prompted.
\item
  \textbf{Opaque:} Reasoning is a "black box."
\item
  \textbf{Expensive:} Requires massive cloud resources.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{The World Model}\label{the-world-model}

Syntra aims for a system that models the world as an \textbf{active
understanding} that evolves with experience. It makes cognition
efficient, predictable, and interpretable, grounded in the fundamental
laws of information physics.

\subsection{3. Non-Goals}\label{non-goals}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Cloud-dependent service architectures.
\item
  Passive-only response systems (reactive vs. proactive).
\item
  Black-box reasoning (all steps must be geometrically traceable).
\item
  Static model updates (Syntra prefers continuous, on-device learning).
\end{itemize}
