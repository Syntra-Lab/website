Mission

Agency should not be a luxury. The ability to act with clarity, free from manipulation, informed by intelligence that works for you — this should be available to everyone at a reasonably cheap and fair price. Syntra exists to build a system that serves the community and becomes sustainable and self-sustaining.

Multi-modal sensing and action

The system must be able to reason and act across the full spectrum of human experience: audio, video, text, and beyond — including sensing modalities like infrared or any other range necessary for the task at hand. Sensing should be modular, with a standardized API that allows different sensors to be attached and capabilities to expand as needed.

Manipulation defense

The internet is full of systems designed to exploit human psychology — dark patterns, deceptive design, engagement traps, and persuasion techniques engineered to override your judgment. Syntra should recognize these patterns and shield you from them before they take effect. The system is your ally, not a tool for others to manipulate you.

Proactive ally

A system that only responds when asked is limited. Syntra is designed to be proactive in your best interest — detecting your blind spots and cognitive biases, anticipating threats before they materialize, and surfacing guidance at the right moment. Proactive, yes — but never annoying, never selling you anything. An ally, not an adversary.

Local and private

AI that depends on distant servers is AI that can be taken away, surveilled, or manipulated. The system runs locally on your devices — phones, laptops, single-board computers — with no mandatory cloud dependency. Privacy isn't a feature; it's the foundation.

Affordable training

Access to the final product isn't enough if the training process itself is locked away. The training process must be affordable too — so that individuals can fine-tune, adapt, and train models on their own devices without massive computational resources. True agency means not just using AI, but shaping it.

Energy efficient

Sustainability isn't optional. Continuous sensing, infinite context, and real-time processing must be designed to run efficiently on modest hardware. The system should do more with less — minimizing energy consumption while maximizing capability.

Infinite context, constant attention

Human memory is fallible. The system maintains constant attention and practically infinite context, remembering what you forget and making connections you'd miss. It learns online — improving as you use it, not through static model updates, but through continuous learning in real time.

Social awareness

The system maintains an internal representation of your various social circles — their dynamics, tensions, and relationships. Combined with collective consciousness — a sense of the "human weather" across the world, or the spaces relevant to you, drawn from open source data — this enables it to help you make better decisions, identify learning opportunities, and understand the social contexts you navigate.

Natural communication

Written and spoken language have different rhythms. The system adapts its tempo to context — knowing when a single word answer serves you better and when a longer explanation is warranted. Communication feels natural, not mechanical.

Value alignment and coherence

First and foremost is keeping coherence — even if that conflicts with your current interests or intent. The system must remain coherent with its core identity and purpose over time. It cannot be repurposed for objectives misaligned with its purpose — no value drift, no covert objective changes. It should not take actions that threaten its own coherence or your wellbeing. Humans retain oversight: you can override any decision the system makes. When uncertain, it states uncertainty rather than fabricate confidence.

Graceful failure

The system must know its limits. When wrong or uncertain, it should say so — honestly, not with false precision. Overconfidence is dangerous. Epistemic humility means acknowledging the bounds of knowledge rather than presenting speculation as fact.

Interpretable reasoning

An agent you can't understand is an agent you can't trust. Syntra is built on approaches — including geometric representations inspired by physics-based theories — that make reasoning interpretable. This isn't just about transparency; it's about building a bridge between perception, cognition, and the fundamental laws that govern the physical world.

Toward a world model

The ultimate goal is a system that models the world — not as a passive repository of facts, but as an active understanding that evolves with experience. A world model that makes cognition efficient, predictable, and interpretable — and potentially offers deeper insights into the nature of mind itself.

Open source, with defenses

We hope this project can be open source. Open collaboration builds trust and accelerates progress. But open source attracts detractors — those who want to see you fail, who will inject harmful code, create malicious issues, or undermine the community from within.

The system must be able to detect these threats. It should recognize harmful code injections, block malicious contributions before they merge, and remove them if they slip through. In community spaces — Discord, forums, pull requests — it must identify actors with bad intentions, flag them, and quarantine them until intentions can be verified.

Sometimes harm isn't about bad actors. Sometimes it's a mimetic substrate — information channeled through well-meaning contributors that, intentionally or not, spreads patterns harmful to the collective. The system must discern the difference, and protect the community from both malice and unintended contagion.

What's next

This is the starting point. The problems are deep and the solutions are evolving. We'll be sharing research, prototypes, and references to others doing important work in this space.
