Why This Matters

AI is becoming essential — but the ability to build and train models is locked behind expensive GPU clusters and specialized knowledge. We believe this should be accessible to everyone.

Whether you're a researcher, developer, student, or curious hobbyist — you should be able to experiment with training your own models.

The Current Problem

To train a model today, you typically need:

  • GPU with 10+ GB VRAM (costs $500-1500+)
  • Cloud compute credits (costs $50-500+ per run)
  • Technical knowledge of frameworks
  • Access to large datasets

This puts meaningful experimentation out of reach for most people. We want to change that.

Our Approach

We're exploring ways to make model training accessible:

1. Efficient Architectures

Researching smaller, more efficient model architectures that can train on consumer hardware.

2. Community Computing

Distributed training approaches where many people contribute small amounts of compute.

3. Transfer Learning

Starting from existing models and fine-tuning for specific use cases — much cheaper than training from scratch.

4. Quantization & Optimization

Making trained models smaller and faster so they can run on lower-end hardware.

How to Participate

This is an open research area. We're documenting our experiments and findings as we go.

  • kalima-ml — Our ML experimentation repo (coming soon)
  • kalima-math — The mathematical foundations we're building on
  • Community — If you're working on similar problems, we'd love to connect

If you have ideas, experiments, or resources to share — reach out. This is bigger than any one project.
